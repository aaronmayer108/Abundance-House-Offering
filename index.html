The Opportunity

We have a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to purchase a property on 160 acres of pristine Anderson Valley wilderness, currently operating as a working wellness and eco-retreat center known as The Land. We are currently raising funds from our community partners to secure this sanctuary for the next generation of retreats, educational initiatives, and shared purpose.

Past gatherings on the property have included retreats and offsites for organizations such as SoulCycle, Louis Vuitton and UCSF.

Due Diligence Highlights

Fully licensed, well managed, fire-ready

A property in good standing with the County of Mendocino, its conservation partners and the local fire district.

Licensed & Permitted

  • County of Mendocino Use Permit UM-44-87/2001/2006 for a visitor service facility is currently entitled and in good standing; renewal in 2033 is described by the former County Chief Planner as a formality.
  • Kitchen and pool permits are in good standing with the Department of Environmental Health.
  • State Water Resources Control Board Domestic Water Supply Permit transfers upon conveyance through the property's certified water tester.
  • Business license reissues to the new owner by simple letter and payment to the county.
  • Diligent 10% TOT and 1% BID tax payer, in good standing with Mendocino County.
  • Conservation easement with the Anderson Valley Land Trust is in good standing, with development rights and protections clearly defined.

Wildfire Resilience

  • Recognized as a model property by the Anderson Valley Fire Department for its wildfire risk-mitigation work.
  • Total insurance costs fell from roughly $22,927/month in 2024 to $17,829/month in 2025 — about a 22% reduction — with a further ~20% decrease reported as of January 2026.
  • Underway: a secondary emergency-egress project renovating Hendy Woods Road — a $700,000 regional effort with state and county partners.
  • Awarded a North Bay Fire Improvement Grant as part of continued fire-safety investment.
Note on wildfire context: Like much of Northern California's wooded terrain, insurers price this region for wildfire exposure — it is not a low-risk zone by nature. What distinguishes Abundance House is the sustained, verifiable mitigation work that has earned it model-property status and measurably falling insurance costs.
